West of Shanghai is a 1937 American adventure film directed by John Farrow and starring Boris Karloff as a Chinese warlord. Other films based on the same play included "The Bad Man" directed by Edwin Carewe and starring Holbrook Blinn, and a 1930 film of the "same title" directed by Clarence Badger and starring Walter Huston. In 1941, M-G-M made a version entitled "The Bad Man", starring Wallace Beery and Ronald Reagan and directed by Richard Thorpe.
