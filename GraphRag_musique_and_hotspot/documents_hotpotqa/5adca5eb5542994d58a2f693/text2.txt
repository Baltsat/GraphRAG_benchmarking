The 1946–47 season was Colchester United's fifth season in their history and their fifth in the Southern League.
 Alongside competing in the Southern League, the club also participated in the FA Cup and Southern League Cup.
 New manager Ted Fenton began to assemble a team of professionals following the reliance on guest players during the 1945–46 season, as the club finished 8th in the league.
 They reached the first round of the FA Cup, but were defeated by Football League side Reading.
 They were also Southern League Cup semi-finalists, defeated at Priestfield Stadium by Gillingham.
