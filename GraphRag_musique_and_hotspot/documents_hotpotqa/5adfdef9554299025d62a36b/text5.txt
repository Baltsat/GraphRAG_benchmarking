 He held that office for only two days, and was known for his opposition to slavery.
 A Whig until the party collapsed, he supported compromise with the south.
 He became an early leader in the Republican Party starting in 1856 serving as the president of the first party convention, delivering its keynote address, and was influential in the nomination of Abraham Lincoln.
