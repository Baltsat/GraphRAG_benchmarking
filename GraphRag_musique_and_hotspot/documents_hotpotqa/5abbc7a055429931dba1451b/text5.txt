Three Colours: Blue (French: Trois couleurs : Bleu ) is a 1993 French drama film directed and co-written by Polish filmmaker Krzysztof Kieślowski.
 "Blue" is the first of three films that comprise the "Three Colours" trilogy, themed on the French Revolutionary ideals of liberty, equality, and fraternity; it is followed by "" and "".
 According to Kieślowski, the subject of the film is liberty, specifically emotional liberty, rather than its social or political meaning.
