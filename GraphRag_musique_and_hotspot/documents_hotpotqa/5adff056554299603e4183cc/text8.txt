Richard W. "Ric" Weiland (April 21, 1953 – June 24, 2006) was a computer software pioneer, programmer and philanthropist.
 He was the second employee at Microsoft Corporation joining the company during his final year at Stanford.
 When he was 35 he left Microsoft to focus his time on investment management and philanthropy, becoming a quiet but well respected donor to the LGBTQ social justice movement, the environment, health & human services and education.
 After his death, the Chronicle of Philanthropy called Ric's bequest the 11th largest charitable gift in the nation with more than $165 million distributed between 20 non profit beneficiaries.
