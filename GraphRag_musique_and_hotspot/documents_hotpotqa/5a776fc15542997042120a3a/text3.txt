The Islamic University of Indonesia (Indonesian: "Universitas Islam Indonesia" or UII , Arabic: الجمعة الاسلامية الاندونيسية) is a private university in Yogyakarta, Indonesia.
 It was established on 27 Rajab 1364 (Islamic calendar) or on 8 July 1945 as STI (Sekolah Tinggi Islam - Islamic Higher School) by political figures of the day including Dr. Muhammad Hatta, Mohammad Natsir, Mohammad Roem, Wahid Hasyim, and Abdul Kahar Muzakkar.
 STI developed into a university called Universitas Islam Indonesia on 14 December 1947.
 Historically, UII is the first national university in Indonesia, and it is the oldest private university in the country.
