Rex Records was a United Kingdom-based record label founded in 1933 by the Crystalate Gramophone Record Manufacturing Company, also the parent of British Imperial Records.
 Rex released their first discs in September 1933, with the initial release bearing a catalogue number of 8000 or 8001.
 The company was taken over by Decca Records in March 1937.
 Rex Records were sold at Marks & Spencer's chain stores.
