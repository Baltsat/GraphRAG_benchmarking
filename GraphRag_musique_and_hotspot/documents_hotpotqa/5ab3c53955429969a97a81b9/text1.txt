David Wilson Jolly (born October 31, 1972) is an American attorney, former lobbyist, and former U.S. Representative for Florida's 13th congressional district.
 A Republican, he previously served as general counsel to his predecessor, U.S. Rep. Bill Young.
 He won the race for Young's seat in a 2014 special election against Democrat Alex Sink.
 He was subsequently re-elected in November 2014, winning 75 percent of the vote, but was unseated in 2016 by former Governor Charlie Crist.
 Jolly has become a prominent Republican critic of U.S. President Donald Trump.
